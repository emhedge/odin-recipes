Double chocolate goji berry cookies

Though the recipe is unique, this image is originally from Nourishing Meals

Decadently delicious balance of sweetness for a health-conscious eater

Somedays, I swear could sit and eat cookies until I make myself sick. Thankfully, my partner is an absolute savant in the kitchen, and she blends a passion and skill for baking that ensures our sweets arsenal is always fully stocked, regardless of my late-night munchies.

One of my favorites, the double chocolate goji berry cookies, is a great option for these days. They're lower in sugar, packed full of goji berries for antioxidant goodness, and to top it off, they're vegan (if that's your thing).

If I talk about this any longer, I may have to walk away from the keyboard and make some myself. Let's get started.

Ingredients

  • 420 grams all-purpose flour
  • 70 grams cocoa powder
  • 2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 tablespoons flaxseed + 6 tablespoons water (to produce 2 flax eggs)
  • 140 grams caster sugar
  • 160 grams coconut or brown sugar
  • 240 grams cold coconut oil or soft butter
  • 100 grams chopped dark chocolate
  • 10 grams goji berries (soaked in water beforehand)

Steps

*First, preheat over to 170 degrees celsius

  1. First, beat up 2 kinds of sugar and cold coconut oil (or soft butter) with a hand mixer until it’s a bit fluffy.
  2. Then add flax eggs and beat up until they’re combined.
  3. Next, sift the flour, cocoa powder, baking powder, and baking soda.
  4. Add the dry ingredients into the wet mixture, along with the chopped chocolate and drained goji berries.
  5. Use a spatula to mix it up until it’s well combined.
  6. After everything is mixed well, shape each piece of cookie dough into a 120g ball.
  7. Lightly press the dough onto the pan before baking.
  8. Bake at 170 degree celsius for 16 min or until cooked through.
