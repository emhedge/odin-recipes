Lean, green, tasty and diverse pesto

Considering pesto is known for being high in fat, perhaps 'lean' isn't the best descriptor for this dish. But, hey, we've learned that most of the original research into fat and its impact on our health was inaccurate, and in fact good fats, like olive oil, can be part of a healthful diet.


Have I bored you to sleep yet? Fear not, I'll move on to the crux of this recipe: the rich, garlicy blend of fresh basil and crunchy walnut. My partner knows I like bold, big flavors, and this dish delivers on that front. Paired with a nice bread, pita, cracker, or even a tortilla, this dish shines and is sure to make you go "mmm" -- you know, the yummy kind of moan.


Despite containing no dairy products, this pesto recipe packs the full pesto punch you expect, and it's surprisingly simple to put together.


Ingredients

  • 1.5kg basil
  • 450 grams garlic
  • 500 grams walnut
  • 3.5 tablespoons salt
  • 1800 grams olive oil


Steps

*To save time, preheat over to 150 degrees celsius

  1. Pick all the basil leaves, drench in boiling water then soak in ice cold water right away. Squeeze out the water.
  2. Lightly roast walnuts for 5 min at 150 degrees celsius.
  3. Blend all the ingredients batch by batch in a blender until smooth.
